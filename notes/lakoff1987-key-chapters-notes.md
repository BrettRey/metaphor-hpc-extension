# Lakoff 1987 - Key Chapters for HPC Reframing

## Overview

This document extracts key material from George Lakoff's *Women, Fire, and Dangerous Things* (1987) relevant to reframing Conceptual Metaphor Theory (CMT) through the lens of Homeostatic Property Cluster (HPC) theory. The central thesis: Boyd argues metaphors succeed by accommodating to causal structure in the world; Lakoff argues they express embodied schemas. HPC theory adds the stability mechanisms that CMT lacks.

**Critical observation:** Lakoff (1987) does NOT cite Boyd. The book was published before Boyd's major HPC papers (1989, 1991, 1999). This means Lakoff's radial categories and image schemas are developed entirely independently of HPC theory, making the convergence all the more striking.

## Chapter 6: Radial Categories (pp. 91-114, lines ~3788-4637)

### Core Argument

Radial categories have a **central member** from which other members are linked via **chaining principles** (domain-of-experience, myth-and-belief, important-property). Members share no common defining properties; instead, they form property clusters held together by experiential domains, cultural knowledge, and metonymies. The category structure is **motivated but not predictable**.

### Key Claims Relevant to HPC Reframing

1. **No common properties required**: "Categories on the whole need not be defined by common properties. There is no reason to believe that the Dyirbal find anything in common among women, fire, dangerous things, etc." (line 3954)

2. **Centrality + chaining structure**: "Centrality: What we have called the basic members of the category are central... Chaining: Complex categories are structured by chaining; central members are linked to other members, which are linked to other members, and so on." (lines 3930-3936)

3. **Motivation vs prediction**: "The general principles given make sense of Dyirbal classification, but they do not predict exactly what the categories will be." (line 3960) — This is the HPC motivation principle: mechanisms explain why the cluster holds, but don't generate the category from scratch.

4. **Domains of experience as organizing principles**: "There are basic domains of experience, which may be culture-specific. These can characterize links in category chains." (lines 3937-3939) — Domains = stabilizing mechanisms in HPC terms.

5. **Idealized models (myths/beliefs)**: "There are idealized models of the world—myths and beliefs among them—that can characterize links in category chains." (lines 3945-3947) — Cultural transmission as a stabilizer.

6. **Specific knowledge overrides general**: (line 3949) — Nearest-neighbor entrenchment beats schematic generalization.

7. **Evidence from language death**: Schmidt's (1985) study of Dyirbal language death shows **peripheral links break off first**, leaving central members. Radial structure collapses from edges inward (lines 3977-4163). This is gradient stability loss—exactly what HPC predicts when stabilizing mechanisms weaken.

### Quotable Passages

> "The analysis of the Dyirbal classifier system... These form what we will be calling a base model. All the base model tells us is that there are four distinctions. Three of them have an internal structure, with elements at the center." (lines 4205-4211)

> "The clearly defined mutually exclusive domains of the base model are consistent with the classical theory of categories. But that does not make it a classical system. This system differs from the classical theory in that it does not have any defining characteristics that are shared by the members of the categories." (lines 4231-4236)

> "This system has the following basic characteristics: motivation. The general principles given make sense of Dyirbal classification, but they do not predict exactly what the categories will be." (lines 3960-3961)

> "Dixon's analysis was not his own. The explanations he gives are just those that native speakers told him about. They are the accounts given by the Dyirbal themselves to explain those parts of their classification system for which they had a conscious explanation." (lines 3988-3991) — Folk causal models align with actual mechanisms.

> "Schmidt has shown that parts of the system, and not just individual cases, are being lost. This is a fact that cannot be explained by the claim that classifiers are mere historical relics, learned one by one, with no cognitively real system." (lines 4550-4554) — Refutes list-learning; supports systemic structure maintained by mechanisms.

### Japanese *hon* Classifier (lines 4260-4509)

Lakoff's analysis of Japanese *hon* (classifier for long, thin objects) shows:

1. **Central members**: rigid long thin objects (sticks, pencils, bamboo)
2. **Extensions via image-schema transformations**: trajectory ↔ long thin object (baseball hits, telephone calls via wires)
3. **Metonymy**: principal functional object → principal goal (bat → hit; needle → injection)
4. **Conventional mental images**: rolls of tape (unrolled = functional = long and thin)
5. **Category chaining**: letters → telephone calls → radio/TV → movies (all communication via CONDUIT metaphor)

**HPC connection:** Image-schema transformations and metonymies are **cognitive mechanisms** that maintain category coherence. The *hon* category is a property cluster (rigid + long + thin + functional salience) held together by embodied image schemas and cultural conventions.

> "Hits in baseball and long, thin objects do not have anything objective in common. The relationship between the bat and the hit is given by an image-schema transformation and a metonymy. Hence, the classical theory, which requires that categorization be based on common properties, is inadequate." (lines 4409-4412)

> "Arbitrariness is a last resort. Even if there are some completely unmotivated cases, one can still apply a slightly weakened criterion of adequacy. Find out which extensions 'make sense' to speakers and which extensions seem 'senseless,' and account for those that make sense." (lines 4424-4428)

### HPC Reframing Connection

Radial categories ARE Homeostatic Property Clusters:

- **Property cluster**: No necessary/sufficient conditions; members share overlapping properties
- **Centrality**: Central members have the most properties; peripheral members fewer (graded membership)
- **Chaining**: Links between members via experiential domains, metonymies, image schemas
- **Mechanisms**:
  - Domain-of-experience principle = functional salience + entrenchment
  - Myth-and-belief principle = cultural transmission + narrative scaffolding
  - Image-schema transformations = embodied cognition (body → mind mapping)
  - Metonymy = associative learning (contiguity → conceptual link)
- **Motivation without prediction**: Mechanisms explain why category holds together, but don't generate exact membership (same as HPC)
- **Failure mode**: Language death shows gradual loss of peripheral members when mechanisms (cultural transmission, daily use) weaken

**Key gap in Lakoff:** No explicit theory of what **maintains** radial structure over time. He describes chaining principles but doesn't theorize stability mechanisms. HPC fills this gap: categories are stable because mechanisms (acquisition, entrenchment, interactive alignment, iterated transmission) continuously regenerate the property cluster.

## Chapter 12: Biological Species Concepts (pp. 187-195, lines ~7231-7596)

### Core Argument

Biological species are **not classical natural kinds**. Post-Darwinian evolutionary biology requires categories that:
- Lack homogeneous internal structure (variation is the rule)
- Are defined relationally (reproductive isolation from other populations)
- Are defined over gene pools, not individual properties
- Have graded boundaries (incipient speciation)
- Are non-transitive (ring species)
- Lack necessary/sufficient conditions (morphology and interbreeding can dissociate)
- Depend on geography and time

Mayr's biological species concept, phenetic approaches (statistical clustering), and cladistic approaches (historical branching) all reject essentialism. The chapter argues objectivist metaphysics is incompatible with evolutionary biology.

### Key Claims Relevant to HPC Reframing

1. **Polythetic definitions replace essentialist definitions**: "Species could no longer be viewed as homogeneous groups of individuals, but as polytypic groups, often with significant subdivisions. Polythetic definitions, in terms of statistically covarying properties, replaced essentialist definitions." (Hull 1984, quoted lines 7306-7309)

2. **Seven ways species fail to be natural kinds** (lines 7332-7494):
   - No homogeneous structure (statistical variation)
   - Defined relationally (reproductive isolation)
   - Not defined by individual properties (gene pool)
   - No clear boundaries (incipient speciation)
   - Non-transitive (ring species: A↔B, B↔C, C↔D, D↔E, but A≠E)
   - No necessary conditions (morphology ≠ interbreeding)
   - Geography-dependent (habitat changes alter species status)

3. **Prototype effects in biology**: "Biological species show prototype effects. Populations that are best examples of the biological species concept have all three of these characteristics [reproductive isolation, ecological difference, morphological distinguishability]. But the biological world is sufficiently complex that a clear same-or-different species judgment cannot be given in a great many cases." (lines 7380-7386)

4. **Phenetic species = dense regions in hyperspace**: "Insistence on a phenetic species concept leads inevitably to a conceptualization of species as dense regions within a hyperdimensional environmental space." (Sokal & Crovello 1984, quoted lines 7525-7527) — This is almost exactly Boyd's HPC formulation (property space clustering).

5. **Emancipation from inappropriate philosophy**: "The development of the biological concept of the species is one of the earliest manifestations of the emancipation of biology from an inappropriate philosophy based on the phenomena of inanimate nature." (Mayr 1984a, quoted line 7328)

### Quotable Passages

> "According to evolutionary biology, species are not natural kinds in the technical objectivist sense, namely, classical categories defined by common essential properties. In fact, species in evolutionary biology are not classical categories at all." (lines 7281-7284)

> "In setting up taxonomies, cladists only count synapomorphies and not autapomorphies. Hence, the surviving descendants of Archosauria, birds and crocodiles are grouped together by cladists; crocodiles are not grouped with other reptiles, even though they are much more like other reptiles today than they are like birds." (lines 7554-7559) — Purpose-relativity: cladistics privileges historical branching over current function.

> "Classical categories and natural kinds are remnants of pre-Darwinian philosophy. They fit the biology of the ancient Greeks very well, and even the biology of local naturalists such as Linnaeus. But they do not accord with phenomena that are central to evolution—variation within species, adaptation to the environment, gradual change, gene pools, etc." (lines 7590-7595)

### HPC Reframing Connection

**Critical observation:** Lakoff does NOT cite Boyd. The book predates Boyd's 1989 "Realism, Anti-Foundationalism, and the Enthusiasm for Natural Kinds." Lakoff is working from Mayr, Hull, Sokal & Crovello, and Gould—all evolutionary biologists.

**What Lakoff is missing:**
- Boyd (1989, 1991, 1999) explicitly argues biological species ARE natural kinds—just not classical ones. They are **Homeostatic Property Cluster kinds**.
- Boyd's HPC theory provides exactly what Lakoff needs: a **non-classical account of natural kinds** that accommodates:
  - Statistical property clustering (phenetic "dense regions")
  - Relational definitions (gene flow, ecological niche)
  - Graded boundaries (incipient speciation)
  - Maintenance mechanisms (selection, gene flow, ecological constraints)

**The reframing argument:**
- Lakoff: "Species are not natural kinds (classical), therefore objectivism fails."
- Boyd: "Species ARE natural kinds (HPC), therefore we need a better theory of natural kinds."
- HPC reframing: "Lakoff's radial categories are linguistic HPCs; biological species are biological HPCs. Both are property clusters maintained by mechanisms. CMT describes the cognitive mechanisms (image schemas, metaphor, metonymy); HPC describes the general framework (property clustering + homeostasis)."

**Boyd's innovation:** He generalized the biological species concept (Mayr, Dobzhansky) into a **general theory of natural kinds**. Lakoff argues species aren't classical natural kinds; Boyd argues they're a *different kind* of natural kind. HPC subsumes both radial categories (linguistic) and species (biological) under one framework.

## Chapters 16-17: Image Schemas and Embodied Meaning (pp. 263-278, lines ~10066-10665)

### Chapter 16: Experiential Realism (lines 10066-10224)

**Core argument:** Putnam's internal realism rejects the "God's Eye View" of objectivism but retains realism. Experiential realism (Lakoff & Johnson) is a version of internal realism that grounds meaning in **embodied experience**:
- Basic-level categories (gestalt perception, motor interaction, mental imagery)
- Kinesthetic image schemas (CONTAINER, PATH, LINK, FORCE, BALANCE, UP-DOWN, etc.)

> "Experiential realism, or experientialism, as Johnson and I (1980) called it, is a version of internal realism that attempts to provide at least some of what is needed: alternative accounts of meaning, truth, knowledge, understanding, objectivity, and reason; a theory of cognitive models capable of dealing with the facts of categorization and natural language semantics." (lines 10207-10213)

**Dual foundations of meaning** (lines 10274-10301):
1. **Basic-level structure**: Defined by convergence of gestalt perception, bodily movement, mental imagery
2. **Kinesthetic image-schematic structure**: CONTAINER, PATH, LINK, FORCE, BALANCE, UP-DOWN, FRONT-BACK, PART-WHOLE, CENTER-PERIPHERY

These structures are **directly meaningful** because they are "directly and repeatedly experienced because of the nature of the body and its mode of functioning in our environment" (lines 10286-10288).

### Chapter 17: Image Schemas as Pre-Conceptual Structure (lines 10350-10665)

**Core claim:** Experience is structured **prior to and independent of concepts**. Image schemas are embodied gestalts that provide:
- **Structural elements** (e.g., CONTAINER has INTERIOR, BOUNDARY, EXTERIOR)
- **Basic logic** (e.g., everything is either inside or outside a container → P or not-P)
- **Metaphorical extensions** (e.g., visual field as CONTAINER, relationships as CONTAINER)

#### Key Image Schemas

1. **CONTAINER schema** (lines 10406-10448)
   - Bodily experience: body as container (ingestion/excretion); rooms, clothes, etc.
   - Structural elements: INTERIOR, BOUNDARY, EXTERIOR
   - Basic logic: P or not-P (in or out); modus ponens (if A in B, X in A → X in B)
   - Metaphors: visual field (come into sight), relationships (trapped in marriage)

2. **PART-WHOLE schema** (lines 10482-10510)
   - Bodily experience: body as whole with manipulable parts
   - Structural elements: WHOLE, PARTS, CONFIGURATION
   - Basic logic: asymmetric (A part of B → B not part of A); irreflexive; configuration matters
   - Metaphors: families, social organizations (marriage creates whole; divorce = splitting up)

3. **LINK schema** (lines 10511-10521)
   - Bodily experience: umbilical cord, holding parents' hands, tying objects together
   - Structural elements: two entities A and B, LINK connecting them
   - Basic logic: constraint and dependence; symmetry (A linked to B ↔ B linked to A)
   - Metaphors: social ties, bondage (slavery), freedom (nothing tying us down)

4. **CENTER-PERIPHERY schema** (lines 10522-10542)
   - Bodily experience: trunk/organs (center) vs. fingers/toes/hair (periphery)
   - Structural elements: ENTITY, CENTER, PERIPHERY
   - Basic logic: periphery depends on center, not vice versa
   - Metaphors: theories have central/peripheral principles; importance = centrality

5. **SOURCE-PATH-GOAL schema** (lines 10543-10574)
   - Bodily experience: every movement has starting point, trajectory, ending point
   - Structural elements: SOURCE, DESTINATION, PATH, DIRECTION
   - Basic logic: passing through intermediate points takes time; further = more time elapsed
   - Metaphors: **PURPOSES ARE DESTINATIONS** (going toward a goal, getting sidetracked, obstacles in the way)

#### Metaphor Motivation (lines 10579-10665)

Lakoff asks three questions about metaphor:
1. What makes a domain appropriate as a **source**?
2. Why is domain X paired with domain Y (and not Z)?
3. Why are specific elements mapped the way they are?

**Answer: Structural correlations in bodily experience**

Example: MORE IS UP; LESS IS DOWN
- Structural correlation: adding water/objects → level rises; removing → level falls
- Correlation motivates every detail of the metaphor (MORE↔UP, LESS↔DOWN)

Example: PURPOSES ARE DESTINATIONS
- Structural correlation: achieving purpose of reaching location B requires moving from A to B
- Pairing in experience: Initial State = Location A; Final State = Location B; Action = Movement
- Metaphorical extension: unfulfilled desire = starting point; desired state = endpoint; actions = movement

> "This correlation provides an answer to questions 2 and 3: VERTICALITY serves as an appropriate source domain for understanding QUANTITY because of the regular correlation in our experience between VERTICALITY and QUANTITY. The details of the mapping are motivated by the details of structural correlation cited above. Every detail of the metaphor is motivated by our physical functioning." (lines 10615-10620)

### Quotable Passages

> "Experience is thus not taken in the narrow sense of the things that have 'happened to happen' to a single individual. Experience is instead construed in the broad sense: the totality of human experience and everything that plays a role in it—the nature of our bodies, our genetically inherited capacities, our modes of physical functioning in the world, our social organization, etc." (lines 10240-10245)

> "Image schemas are relatively simple structures that constantly recur in our everyday bodily experience: CONTAINERS, PATHS, LINKS, FORCES, BALANCE, and in various orientations and relations: UP-DOWN, FRONT-BACK, PART-WHOLE, CENTER-PERIPHERY, etc." (lines 10279-10281)

> "These structures are directly meaningful, first, because they are directly and repeatedly experienced because of the nature of the body and its mode of functioning in our environment." (lines 10286-10289)

> "On our account, the CONTAINER schema is inherently meaningful to people by virtue of their bodily experience. The schema has a meaningful configuration, from which the basic logic follows." (lines 10472-10475)

> "To show that the metaphor is natural in that it is motivated by the structure of our experience, we need to answer three questions: (1) What determines the choice of a possible well-structured source domain? (2) What determines the pairing of the source domain with the target domain? (3) What determines the details of the source-to-target mapping?" (lines 10580-10586)

### HPC Reframing Connection

**Image schemas are cognitive stabilizing mechanisms.**

Lakoff describes image schemas as:
- Embodied (grounded in bodily experience)
- Pre-conceptual (structure experience before concepts)
- Repeatedly experienced (constant exposure)
- Well-structured (gestalt configurations with internal logic)
- Metaphorically productive (project from concrete to abstract)

In HPC terms, image schemas are **acquisition and entrenchment mechanisms**:

1. **Acquisition**: Schemas emerge from repeated bodily experience (CONTAINER from being in rooms, ingesting/excreting; PATH from locomotion; LINK from umbilical cord, holding hands).

2. **Entrenchment**: Constant repetition makes schemas automatic, unconscious, and projectable. "Basic logic" emerges from gestalt structure (CONTAINER → P or not-P is entrenched, not deduced).

3. **Interactive alignment**: Metaphors succeed because they exploit shared embodied schemas. Speakers align on PURPOSES ARE DESTINATIONS because both have experienced achieving purposes via locomotion.

4. **Iterated transmission**: Image schemas are culturally universal (or near-universal) because they're grounded in human body structure + gravity + locomotion. They're transmitted not as explicit knowledge but as structured experience.

**Boyd's accommodation principle:**
> "Metaphors succeed by accommodating to causal structure in the world."

**Lakoff's embodiment principle:**
> "Metaphors succeed by projecting embodied image schemas onto abstract domains."

**HPC synthesis:**
Image schemas ARE causal structures—cognitive structures that emerge from causal interactions (body + environment + repeated experience). Metaphors accommodate to image-schematic structure, which is itself a product of embodied causal regularities. Boyd says metaphors track world structure; Lakoff says metaphors track body-environment structure. Both are right—the body is part of the world, and embodied experience is causally structured.

**What Lakoff is missing:**
- No explicit theory of **stability over time**. Image schemas are described as recurrent structures, but Lakoff doesn't explain what maintains their stability across individuals, communities, and historical change.
- No theory of **projection beyond metaphor**. Image schemas ground metaphor, but do they ground other forms of categorization? (Yes: radial categories use domains of experience, which are image-schematically structured.)
- No theory of **failure modes**. What happens when image schemas conflict (CONTAINER logic vs. LINK logic)? When do they fail to extend productively?

**What HPC adds:**
- Stability mechanisms: image schemas are stable because they're continuously regenerated by bodily experience + cultural transmission
- Projectibility: image schemas support induction because they track reliable body-environment regularities
- Failure modes: image schemas fail when bodily experience changes (microgravity, prosthetics, virtual environments) or when metaphorical extensions become too distant from source domain (dead metaphors, literary coinages)

## PATH Schema Specifically (for TRAIL/PATH Connection)

### Definition (lines 10543-10559)

**SOURCE-PATH-GOAL schema:**
- **Bodily experience**: "Every time we move anywhere there is a place we start from, a place we wind up at, a sequence of contiguous locations connecting the starting and ending points, and a direction."
- **Structural elements**: SOURCE (starting point), DESTINATION (end point), PATH (sequence of contiguous locations), DIRECTION (toward destination)
- **Basic logic**: If you go from source to destination, you must pass through each intermediate point; the further along the path, the more time has elapsed.
- **Metaphors**: PURPOSES ARE DESTINATIONS (achieving goals = moving along path); COMPLEX EVENTS ARE PATHS (initial state = source, intermediate stages = path, final state = destination)

### TRAIL as PATH Variant

Lakoff does not use the word "trail" in this book. However, the TRAIL metaphor (argued in Brett's paper?) would be an elaboration of PATH schema:

- **PATH**: abstract schema (sequence of contiguous locations)
- **TRAIL**: specific instantiation (marks left by repeated movement; self-reinforcing; visible record of prior passage)

HPC connection: A trail is a PATH maintained by a homeostatic mechanism (repeated use reinforces the path; abandonment → erosion). This is exactly the HPC structure: property cluster (cleared ground, packed earth, visible markers) maintained by mechanism (repeated traversal). Categories are like trails: property clusters maintained by repeated use (entrenchment, transmission).

## Summary: HPC Reframing of Lakoff 1987

### What Lakoff Gets Right (Proto-HPC Insights)

1. **Property clusters without necessary/sufficient conditions**: Radial categories have no common properties; members are linked by overlapping similarities (Wittgensteinian family resemblance).

2. **Centrality + graded structure**: Central members have most properties; peripheral members fewer. Membership is graded, not binary.

3. **Chaining via domains of experience**: Links between members are motivated by experiential domains, myths/beliefs, functional salience, image-schema transformations, metonymies.

4. **Motivation without prediction**: Principles explain why category makes sense but don't generate exact membership. Same as HPC: mechanisms explain clustering but don't predict all details.

5. **Evidence from language change**: Dyirbal language death shows radial structure collapses from periphery inward when cultural transmission weakens. Exactly the gradient stability loss HPC predicts.

6. **Embodied grounding**: Image schemas are directly meaningful because they're grounded in repeated bodily experience. They provide pre-conceptual structure for metaphorical extension.

7. **Structural correlations motivate metaphor**: Metaphors succeed because they map onto experiential correlations (MORE↔UP because adding substance raises level). Motivation, not arbitrariness.

### What Lakoff Is Missing (What HPC Adds)

1. **No theory of stability mechanisms**: Lakoff describes radial structure and image schemas but doesn't explain what **maintains** them over time. HPC fills this gap: categories are stable because mechanisms (acquisition, entrenchment, interactive alignment, iterated transmission) continuously regenerate the property cluster.

2. **No theory of projectibility**: Why do some extensions "make sense" and others don't? Lakoff says "motivation" but doesn't explain when motivation supports induction. HPC answer: motivated extensions are projectable when they track reliable mechanisms.

3. **No theory of failure modes**: When do radial categories break down? When do image schemas fail to extend? Lakoff documents language death but doesn't theorize failure. HPC provides failure typology: thin clustering (no mechanism), fat clustering (mechanisms conflict), negative diagnosis (defined by absence).

4. **No connection to natural kinds**: Lakoff argues species aren't classical natural kinds and concludes objectivism fails. Boyd argues species ARE natural kinds—just HPC kinds, not classical ones. Lakoff rejects the wrong target; he should have argued for non-classical natural kinds (which is exactly what Boyd did in 1989).

5. **No integration across domains**: Lakoff treats linguistic categories (radial structure) and biological categories (species) separately. HPC unifies them: both are property clusters maintained by mechanisms. Linguistic mechanisms = image schemas, metonymy, cultural transmission. Biological mechanisms = gene flow, selection, ecological constraints.

### The HPC Reframing Argument

**Thesis:** Lakoff's Conceptual Metaphor Theory (CMT) describes **cognitive stabilizing mechanisms** for linguistic categories. Boyd's HPC theory provides the general framework. Image schemas, metonymy, and metaphorical projection are the mechanisms that maintain linguistic property clusters; gene flow, selection, and ecological constraints maintain biological property clusters. Same structure, different mechanisms.

**Convergence without citation:** Lakoff (1987) and Boyd (1989) developed nearly identical anti-essentialist frameworks independently:
- Lakoff: radial categories = property clusters linked by motivated extensions
- Boyd: HPC kinds = property clusters maintained by homeostatic mechanisms
- Neither cites the other; both cite evolutionary biology (Mayr, Dobzhansky, Hull)

**The synthesis:**
- **Boyd**: "Metaphors accommodate to causal structure in the world."
- **Lakoff**: "Metaphors express embodied image schemas."
- **HPC synthesis**: Image schemas ARE causal structures—cognitive patterns that emerge from causal interactions (body + environment + repeated experience). Metaphors accommodate to image-schematic structure, which is causally grounded in bodily experience.

**What HPC adds to CMT:**
- Stability theory: Image schemas are stable because they're continuously regenerated by bodily experience (acquisition + entrenchment)
- Projectibility theory: Image schemas support induction because they track reliable body-environment regularities
- Failure-mode theory: Image schemas fail when bodily experience changes (microgravity, prosthetics) or when extensions become too distant (dead metaphors)
- Cross-domain generalization: Same HPC structure applies to linguistic categories (image schemas), biological categories (gene flow), social categories (norms + sanctions), and artifactual categories (function + design constraints)

**The paper's move:** Lakoff developed CMT to replace objectivism but lacked a stability theory. Boyd developed HPC to generalize biological species concepts but lacked a cognitive implementation. Putting them together: CMT specifies the mechanisms (image schemas, metonymy, metaphor) that HPC abstractly characterizes (property clustering + homeostasis). Radial categories are linguistic HPCs.
