# Paper Outline: What Dogs Know About Trails

**Target:** ~8,500 words. *Mind & Language* or *Cognitive Science*.

**Thesis:** HPC theory reframes Conceptual Metaphor Theory by supplying the stability mechanisms that Lakoff & Johnson's account lacks. Metaphor = cross-domain HPC extension on partial property overlap. This operation is phylogenetically old; what's distinctively human is deliberate exploitation, recursion, and communication.

---

## Section 1: Introduction (~800 words)

**Function:** Establish the white space and state the thesis.

**Key moves:**
- CMT (Lakoff & Johnson 1980; Lakoff 1987) and HPC theory (Boyd 1979, 1999) developed in parallel without engagement
- Both reject classical categories, both take fuzzy boundaries seriously, but their explanatory architectures differ fundamentally
- Lakoff (1987) doesn't cite Boyd; both cite evolutionary biology (Mayr, Dobzhansky, Hull)
- White space confirmed: Boyd not cited in experimental metaphor literature (Flusberg, Thibodeau)
- Thesis: HPC supplies what CMT lacks (mechanisms, stability, falsifiability) and metaphor supplies what HPC hasn't addressed (cross-domain extension)
- Preview the dog TRAIL case as vivid illustration

**Citations:** Lakoff & Johnson 1980, Lakoff 1987, Boyd 1979, Flusberg et al. 2018/2024, Thibodeau et al. 2019

## Section 2: Source Domains Without Mechanisms (~1,200 words)

**Function:** Exposition of CMT showing that L&J's source domains have HPC structure but lack the stability story.

**Key moves:**
- L&J's experiential gestalts ARE property clusters (the 12-property causation example, L&J 1980: 69-72)
- Image schemas (CONTAINER, PATH, LINK, PART-WHOLE) as recurring bodily structures (Lakoff 1987: lines 10279-10289)
- Radial categories = property clusters linked by motivated extensions (Lakoff 1987: Dyirbal, lines 3930-3960)
- Partial mapping is central to L&J, not an embarrassment (L&J 1980: 13; 2003 Afterword: 254)
- Multiple metaphors for one concept (LOVE IS JOURNEY + WAR + PATIENT) = Murphy's (1996: 186) incoherence problem under strong CMT
- 2003 Afterword stability mechanisms (neural recruitment, Hebbian learning) are a start but lack the causal-ecological layer
- The gap stated precisely: Lakoff has phenomenology without etiology. No theory of what maintains clustering across speakers, time, generations.
- Language death evidence: Dyirbal peripheral links break off first (Schmidt 1985 via Lakoff 1987: lines 3977-4163)

**Citations:** Lakoff & Johnson 1980/2003, Lakoff 1987, Murphy 1996, Glynn 2010

## Section 3: HPC and Theory-Constitutive Metaphor (~1,200 words)

**Function:** Introduce Boyd's apparatus: HPC kinds, TCMs, accommodation, catachresis.

**Key moves:**
- Boyd's TCMs: metaphorical expressions that constitute irreplaceable parts of scientific theory (Boyd 1979: 482)
- Accommodation to causal structure (Boyd 1979: 483): language has to "cut the world at its joints," but joints are context-relative
- HPC kinds: property clusters maintained by homeostatic mechanisms (Boyd 1979: 526-527, the 10-point characterization) -- present 3-4 key features, not all 10
- Inductive open-endedness: TCMs are "invitations to future research" (Boyd 1979: 489)
- Catachresis: TCMs fill genuine vocabulary gaps (Boyd 1979: 481-482)
- Precision through vagueness (Boyd 1979: 530): extensional vagueness reflects accurate accommodation to HPC structure
- The reflexivity: HPC applied to grammar IS a theory-constitutive metaphor in Boyd's sense

**Citations:** Boyd 1979, Cummiskey 1992, Field 1973

## Section 4: Metaphor as Cross-Domain HPC Extension (~1,500 words)

**Function:** The theoretical reframing. This is the paper's core contribution.

**Key moves:**
- The proposal: metaphor = extending an HPC-structured category to instances in a different domain where only some homeostatic properties are present
- Source and target domains are both HPC kinds
- What HPC adds to metaphor theory:
  - Stability mechanisms (why metaphors persist)
  - Falsification structure (thin/fat/negative failure modes)
  - Graded membership grounded in mechanisms (not cognitive illusions)
- What metaphor adds to HPC:
  - Cross-domain extension (HPC has focused on within-domain categorisation)
  - Gradience at the literal/metaphorical boundary (itself a fuzzy boundary, as HPC predicts)
- Three debates reframed:
  1. Comparison vs categorisation (Gentner vs Glucksberg) = stages of HPC extension
  2. Attribute vs relational (Murphy, Gentner) = relations are mechanisms; systematicity = homeostatic coherence
  3. Embodied vs abstract (Lakoff vs Murphy/Gentner) = embodied schemas are one mechanism type among several
- Success/failure conditions (Cummiskey 1992: caloric vs kinetic theory -- mechanism articulability discriminates)
- Boyd explains WHY metaphors succeed (causal structure); Lakoff explains HOW they arise (embodiment). HPC fills both gaps.

**Citations:** Boyd 1979, Gentner 1983, Bowdle & Gentner 2005, Murphy 1996, Holyoak & Stamenkovic 2018, Cummiskey 1992

## Section 5: What Dogs Know About Trails (~2,000 words)

**Function:** The empirical case study. Vivid, escalating, stripped of deflation.

**Subsection 5.1: TRAIL as HPC**
- The property cluster: 7 properties (linear extent, visual contrast, hard substrate, concentrated scent, frequent locomotion, connectivity, bounded width)
- Causal-ecological maintenance: trails get hard and scent-rich because they're used; they're used because they connect locations
- Evidence from three studies:
  - Thesen 1993: three-phase tracking (search/decide/track), 100% success, 6 Hz sniff frequency, active structure extraction
  - Hepper & Wells 2005: 5 sequential footsteps required; randomised footsteps = failure; individual odour, not ground disturbance
  - Cafazzo 2012: crossroad marking at territorial boundaries (z = 2.98, p = 0.003); functional topology of trail network

**Subsection 5.2: The continuum from trail to "career path"**
- The table: well-worn trail → paved sidewalk → painted line → line of ants → "career path"
- Properties present decreasing along the continuum
- The painted-line observation: strips away scent, substrate, energy advantage, human to follow
- What remains: only visual-geometric properties (linear, bounded, high-contrast)
- The painted line belongs to a different functional domain (spatial demarcation, not locomotion)
- Cross-domain recruitment on partial property overlap = the metaphorical move
- Remaining deflation (edge-following / thigmotaxis) and why it fails

**Subsection 5.3: Wild canids confirm the category**
- Wolves mark ON trails; pets avoid contaminating trails; both require TRAIL/non-TRAIL discrimination
- Cafazzo 2012 bridges wild and domestic
- Context modulation: same animal, different rules depending on functional context (den-protection vs territorial signalling)
- Monclús et al.: wolf-dog convergence in scat patterns

**Citations:** Thesen et al. 1993, Hepper & Wells 2005, Cafazzo et al. 2012, Reddy et al. 2021, Vilà et al., Barja et al., Monclús et al.

## Section 6: Phylogeny and What's Distinctively Human (~1,200 words)

**Function:** Draw out the phylogenetic implication and distinguish human metaphor from the basic operation.

**Key moves:**
- If metaphor = cross-domain HPC extension on partial property overlap, then any animal with HPC-structured categories should show cross-domain recruitment when perceptual overlap triggers it
- The boundary between "broad literal category" and "metaphorical extension" is itself fuzzy (an HPC prediction)
- Other possible cases: birds mobbing owl decoys, corvid tool use -- but diagnostic difficulty (same vs different functional domain)
- What's distinctively human: not the basic operation but deliberate exploitation, recursive stacking ("career path" → "roadblock on my career path" → "detour around the roadblock"), linguistic communication of the mapping
- The career of metaphor (Bowdle & Gentner 2005) as HPC stabilisation trajectory: novel → conventional → dead
- Conventionalisation = mechanism generalisation; explains why some metaphors conventionalise and others don't
- Noyes & Keil 2019: generics designate kinds (inductive potential) without essences -- cultural transmission as HPC stabiliser

**Citations:** Bowdle & Gentner 2005, Holyoak & Stamenkovic 2018, Noyes & Keil 2019

## Section 7: Implications and Conclusion (~800 words)

**Function:** Sketch the broader implications and close.

**Key moves:**
- For metaphor theory: HPC gives CMT causal grounding, stability explanation, projectibility account, failure conditions, and field-relativity
- For HPC theory: metaphor shows HPC-structured categories reaching across domains, a capacity not previously theorised
- The testability asymmetry: Lakoff's ICMs are hard to falsify (Glynn 2010/2015); HPC gives falsification structure
- The reflexivity restated: this paper's own use of HPC to reframe CMT is a theory-constitutive metaphor in Boyd's sense
- Return to the dog: the simplest version of the argument is that a dog following a painted line shows us what metaphor is before language complicates it
- Open questions: Can aptness be operationalised as mechanism transferability? Does the account extend to conceptual blending (Fauconnier & Turner)?

**Citations:** Boyd 1979, Glynn 2010, Lakoff 1987
