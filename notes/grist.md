# Metaphor as Cross-Domain HPC Extension

## Working title
"What Dogs Know About Trails: Metaphor, Mechanisms, and the Phylogeny of Cross-Domain Extension"

Alternative: "Categories Without Cognitivism: How HPC Theory Reframes Conceptual Metaphor"

## Origin
Two Many Minds podcast episodes (Flusberg on metaphor; Mitchell on AI/ARC). Session 2026-02-26. The Flusberg episode surfaced Boyd's theory-constitutive metaphors (TCMs); the Mitchell episode surfaced same-extension-different-mechanism phenomena. These converged with dog TRAIL observations into a unified argument.

**Terminological convention:** TRAIL = the dog's concrete ecological category (a physical feature of the landscape with clustered properties maintained by use). PATH = Talmy/Lakoff's abstract linguistic schema (source-trajectory-goal). The distinction does work in the argument: dogs have TRAIL; humans abstract PATH from TRAIL and extend it metaphorically ("career path").

---

## The white space

No published work directly uses HPC theory to critique Lakoff's conceptual metaphor theory or cognitive linguistics more broadly. The two literatures developed in parallel without engagement. Boyd (HPC, TCMs) and Lakoff (CMT, ICMs) both reject classical categories, both take fuzzy boundaries seriously, but their explanatory architectures are fundamentally different. Nobody has articulated this.

#### Added: The convergence without citation

**Critical observation:** Lakoff (1987) does NOT cite Boyd. The book was published before Boyd's major HPC papers (1989, 1991, 1999). This means Lakoff's radial categories and image schemas are developed entirely independently of HPC theory, making the convergence all the more striking.

**Convergence without citation:** Lakoff (1987) and Boyd (1989) developed nearly identical anti-essentialist frameworks independently:
- Lakoff: radial categories = property clusters linked by motivated extensions
- Boyd: HPC kinds = property clusters maintained by homeostatic mechanisms
- Neither cites the other; both cite evolutionary biology (Mayr, Dobzhansky, Hull)

**White space confirmed (from paper mining):** Boyd is not cited in any of the three Flusberg/Thibodeau papers. The experimental metaphor literature and the HPC/philosophy-of-science literature have no published intersection.

Closest approaches:
- Miller (2021): words as HPC natural kinds (implicitly challenges prototype-based accounts of word meaning)
- Glynn (2015): ICMs "produce results that are not systematically falsifiable"
- Critiques of Lakoff from within linguistics (embodiment claims, CMT conflates pre- and post-metaphoric structure) -- but none from an HPC direction

## Two interlocking arguments

### Argument A: HPC reframes Lakoff & Johnson

**The claim:** L&J's source domains are property clusters without stability mechanisms. HPC supplies the mechanisms. The reframing preserves everything descriptively valuable in CMT while relocating the explanation from embodied cognition to causal-ecological structure.

#### Added from Boyd 1979 notes

**Boyd's 10-point HPC characterization (pp. 526-527):**
1. **Family of clustered properties:** There is a family F of properties contingently clustered in nature
2. **Homeostatic co-occurrence:** Their co-occurrence is "the result of what may be metaphorically (sometimes literally) described as a sort of *homeostasis*" – mechanisms favor presence of some properties given others, or maintain presence together
3. **Causal importance:** "The homeostatic clustering of the properties in F is causally important: there are (theoretically or practically) important effects which are produced by a conjoint occurrence of (many of) the properties in F together with (some or all of) the underlying mechanisms in question"
4. **Kind term application:** There is a kind term *t* applied to things in which the homeostatic clustering of most properties in F occurs
5. **Natural definition:** "It is an analytic definition; rather all or most of the homeostatic cluster F together with some or all of the mechanisms that underlie it provide the natural definition of *t*"
6. **Imperfect homeostasis:** "Homeostasis is nomologically possible or actual: some thing may display some but not all of the properties in F; some but not all of the relevant underlying homeostatic mechanisms may be present"
7. **Relative importance varies:** The relative importance of properties and mechanisms in determining kind membership "is a theoretical rather than a conceptual issue"
8. **Extensional vagueness:** "There will be many cases of extensional vagueness that are not resolvable even given all the relevant facts and all the true theories"
9. **Causal importance of clustering:** The clustering F + mechanisms together "is such that the kind or property denoted by *t* is a natural kind reference important for scientific explanation or for the formulation of successful inductive inferences"
10. **No refinement preserves naturalness:** No refinement replacing *t* with a more extensionally precise term "will preserve the naturalness of the kind referred to"

**Historicity claim (Boyd p. 527):** "The homeostatic property cluster which serves to define *t* is not individuated extensionally. Instead, the property cluster is an individuated like a (type or token) historical object or process: certain changes over time (or in space) in the property cluster or in the underlying homeostatic mechanisms preserve the identity of the defining cluster." This means categories can change while remaining the same category.

**Why TCMs can't be fully explicated (Boyd p. 529):** "Where a theory-constitutive metaphor (or any other expression) refers to such a phenomenon, there is no reason in general to believe that such an explication is even possible. The properties that constitute the homeostatic property cluster may not even be finite in number and they may vary significantly from time to time or place to place, so that a finite characterization of them (much less a cognitively tractable characterization) need not be possible." This is the anti-empiricist punch line: we cannot have precise definitions precisely because natural kinds are HPC phenomena.

#### Added from Lakoff 1987 notes

**Radial category structure (lines 3930-3936):** "Centrality: What we have called the basic members of the category are central... Chaining: Complex categories are structured by chaining; central members are linked to other members, which are linked to other members, and so on."

**Motivation vs prediction (line 3960):** "The general principles given make sense of Dyirbal classification, but they do not predict exactly what the categories will be." This is the HPC motivation principle: mechanisms explain why the cluster holds, but don't generate the category from scratch.

**Evidence from language death (Schmidt 1985, cited lines 3977-4163):** Dyirbal language death shows **peripheral links break off first**, leaving central members. Radial structure collapses from edges inward. This is gradient stability loss—exactly what HPC predicts when stabilizing mechanisms weaken.

**Folk causal models (lines 3988-3991):** "Dixon's analysis was not his own. The explanations he gives are just those that native speakers told him about. They are the accounts given by the Dyirbal themselves to explain those parts of their classification system for which they had a conscious explanation." Folk causal models align with actual mechanisms.

**Systemic structure required (lines 4550-4554):** "Schmidt has shown that parts of the system, and not just individual cases, are being lost. This is a fact that cannot be explained by the claim that classifiers are mere historical relics, learned one by one, with no cognitively real system." Refutes list-learning; supports systemic structure maintained by mechanisms.

**The key distinction (Boyd vs. Lakoff on metaphor):**
- Boyd: metaphors succeed because they *accommodate to causal structure in the world*. TCMs are referentially successful -- they latch onto genuine causal-structural similarities between domains. The success is explained by the world's structure.
- Lakoff: metaphors work because they express *embodied schemas*. The ubiquity of everyday metaphors is explained by the body's sensorimotor structure. The success is explained by the mind's structure.
- These are substantially different claims about what metaphors are *for* and why they work. You can't split the difference.

**Boyd on why accommodation matters (p. 510):**
"What I am calling 'accommodation' is a response to an epistemological problem, whose discovery represents the principal epistemological achievement of early empiricism: inductive generalization is reliable (or, at any rate, is nonaccidentally reliable) only if the categories in terms of which generalizations are formulated correspond in the right way to the causal powers of things in the material world."

**Boyd on reference dynamics (p. 504):**
"Reference has an essential dynamic and dialectical aspect. Changes in language use – when they reflect the dialectics of accommodation – do not represent changes of reference in any philosophically puzzling sense of that term. Instead, such dialectical changes of reference are characteristic of referential continuity and represent perfectly ordinary vehicles for the reporting of new discoveries."

**Boyd on epistemic access vs linguistic accommodation (pp. 504-505):**
"The accommodation of our language and conceptual categories to the causal structure of the world ('cutting the world at its joints') is essential in order that tolerance be possible. Since – in the absence of perfect causal knowledge – such accommodation cannot be accomplished by explicit and conventional definitions, nondefinitional procedures for accommodating language to the world are essential to knowledge. Since knowledge gathering is the essential core of reference, *the processes of linguistic accommodation are essential components of reference*."

**The catachresis point (Boyd):** TCMs are catachrestic -- they fill a genuine vocabulary gap where no literal expression exists. "Homeostatic property cluster" when applied to grammatical categories is catachrestic in exactly this sense. There was no literal term for "a category constituted by co-occurring properties sustained by causal mechanisms that tolerates fuzzy boundaries." "Prototype" was the closest, but it smuggles in Lakoff's cognitive framing.

**Boyd on catachresis (p. 481):** Black's term "catachresis" = "the use of metaphor to remedy gaps in vocabulary". Boyd argues TCMs fill vocabulary gaps but this is NOT a "substitute for formal comparison" – it has "its own distinctive capacities and merits" (p. 482). The inability to specify similarity/analogy is NOT a defect – it reflects "the necessity of obtaining a *priori* between scientific language and a messy and complex world" (p. 483).

**The reflexivity:** HPC is itself a theory-constitutive metaphor when applied to grammar. The book's entire research programme has this reflexive structure -- importing inferential machinery (homeostasis, property co-occurrence, fuzzy boundaries, causal mechanisms) from biology into linguistics. The metaphor does constitutive work: it makes certain questions askable (What are the homeostatic mechanisms? Why do these properties cluster?) that weren't askable under classical-category or prototype-category frameworks. The partiality of the mapping is where the theoretical work happens.

**Boyd on TCMs as conceits (pp. 487-488):**
TCMs are "conceits," not metaphors in the literary sense. They extend "not through one literary work, but through the work of a generation or more of scientists" and become "the property of the entire scientific community."

**Canonical examples from cognitive psychology (pp. 486-487):**
- Thought as "information processing," brain as "computer"
- Mental states as "preprogrammed" vs. acquired through learning
- Memory as having "storage," "indexing," "retrieval"
- Consciousness as "feedback"
- Learning as response of a "self-organizing machine"

**Boyd on why TCMs are necessary (pp. 495-496):**
"The introduction of theoretical terms does require, however, some tentative or preliminary indication of the properties of the presumed kinds in question. Any such terminology must possess a sort of programmatic open-endedness... Theory-constitutive metaphors, then, simply represent one strategy among many for the preliminary stages of theory construction."

Metaphor is **necessary** when:
- We need to refer to natural kinds before fully understanding their properties
- The kinds in question are HPC phenomena (no complete literal definition possible)
- We need open-ended terms that support discovery of further properties

**The testability asymmetry:** L&J's ICMs are notoriously hard to falsify (Glynn 2015). HPC gives falsification structure: if you can't identify mechanisms, or if the cluster doesn't project, you don't have a category. That's Ch 8's failure modes (thin, fat, negative). Lakoff has no equivalent -- there's no principled way to say "this isn't a radial category" in his framework.

**Lakoff on arbitrariness (lines 4424-4428):** "Arbitrariness is a last resort. Even if there are some completely unmotivated cases, one can still apply a slightly weakened criterion of adequacy. Find out which extensions 'make sense' to speakers and which extensions seem 'senseless,' and account for those that make sense." This shows Lakoff recognizes the need for exclusion power but doesn't provide mechanisms to ground it.

**Rapoport compliance:** Lakoff was right that categories aren't classical, right that metaphor does cognitive work, right that embodiment matters for some aspects of categorization -- but wrong that the structure of linguistic categories is *constituted* by metaphorical cognition rather than *tracked* by it.

### Argument B: Metaphor is phylogenetically old

**The claim:** If metaphor is cross-domain HPC extension on partial property overlap, then any animal with HPC-structured categories should be capable of metaphorical behaviour when perceptual overlap triggers cross-domain recruitment. The basic operation is phylogenetically old; what's distinctively human is deliberate exploitation, recursion, and communication.

**The reframing:** Metaphor = extending an HPC-structured category to instances where only some homeostatic properties are present and the functional ones are absent. The boundary between "broad category" and "metaphorical extension" is itself fuzzy -- it's a matter of how many properties are instantiated and which ones.

## The dog TRAIL case

### The category TRAIL as HPC

Properties that typically cluster in trails:
- Linear extent
- Visual contrast with surrounding ground
- Hard/smooth substrate
- Concentrated conspecific scent
- Frequent human/animal locomotion
- Connectivity between locations (source-trajectory-goal structure)
- Bounded width

These co-occur reliably because of real causal-ecological structure: paths get hard and scent-rich *because* they're used; they get used *because* they connect locations; they become visually distinct *because* of substrate compaction and vegetation wear.

### Three observations with escalating evidential force

**Observation 1: Dogs preferentially follow paths.**
- Real and observable.
- But deflationary alternatives available: energy efficiency (lower locomotion cost on hard substrate), scent-trail following, social referencing (following human who stays on the trail).
- Evidential force: suggestive but underdetermined.

#### Added from Cafazzo 2012 (free-ranging dogs, Rome)

**Crossroads as strategic marking locations:**
Free-ranging dogs concentrate scent-marking at trail crossroads. Males scent-marked via raised-leg urination "mainly at the crossroads along territorial borders (t = 5.00, z = 2.98, n = 16, p = 0.003)" (Cafazzo et al. 2012, p. 960). This is critical: crossroads are by definition locations where trails intersect. The preferential marking at these sites suggests dogs recognize them as functionally significant within their territory's pathway network, not just as arbitrary spatial locations.

**Spatial discrimination data (282.53h observation, 782 urinations):**
- Territorial boundaries vs. inside territory: t = 0.00, z = 3.51, p = 0.0004
- Outside territory vs. territorial boundaries: t = 0.00, z = 3.52, p = 0.0004
- Alpha male defecation: 81.82% (9/11 events) deposited at boundaries/crossroads, 77.78% on conspicuous or high substrates (rocks, plants)

The boundaries themselves function as trails (narrow linear features repeatedly traversed), and crossroads are topological nodes within the trail network. Dogs don't just use trails—they mark trails strategically, discriminating trail junctions from other locations.

**Observation 2: Dogs avoid defecating on paths.**
- Initially explained by vulnerability avoidance (exposed during elimination, prefer sheltered locations) or substrate preference (grass over hardpack).
- **Brett's counter:** Even beside an open soccer field, a dog leaves the trail to defecate on the grass. No vulnerability advantage -- equally exposed in both locations. So the behaviour is specifically "leave the trail," not "seek cover."
- Remaining deflation: substrate preference (grass vs. hardpack). But dogs also step off paved trails onto adjacent non-grass surfaces, tracking the trail/non-trail boundary more than the substrate boundary.
- Evidential force: moderately strong. Requires a representation that discriminates trail from non-trail.
- Evolutionary source: fixed behavioural rule -- move off the high-traffic corridor before eliminating. Adaptive in ancestral environment (parasite transmission reduction, predator avoidance on travel routes, scent-mark placement at margins for communicative value). Rule fires regardless of whether the specific context makes it adaptive.

**Observation 3: Brett's dog follows painted white lines on soccer fields.**
- This is the clincher. Strips away nearly every deflationary alternative simultaneously:
  - No scent concentration on painted lines
  - No substrate difference (paint on grass)
  - No energy advantage
  - No human to follow
- The painted line shares *only* the visual-geometric properties of a trail: linear, bounded, high-contrast strip extending through space.
- Remaining alternative: general edge-following / contrast-tracking behaviour (cf. thigmotaxis in rodents). But edge-following involves walking *alongside* a boundary, not *on* it. If the dog tracks along the line itself, that's "this is a trail, follow it."
- **Key point:** The dog has generalized TRAIL from its typical perceptual correlates to a novel instance sharing only the visual signature. It has abstracted away from scent and substrate to the geometric essence.
- Evidential force: strong. The painted line belongs to a different functional domain (spatial demarcation, not locomotion). The dog is recruiting TRAIL to assimilate an object whose geometric profile fits but whose ecological role doesn't. That cross-domain recruitment is what makes it metaphorical.

### The continuum prediction

If metaphor = cross-domain HPC extension on partial property overlap, then there should be a continuum from literal to metaphorical:

| Instance | Properties present | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Well-worn trail through woods | Nearly all | Core TRAIL |
| Paved sidewalk beside soccer field | Most (visual, substrate, traffic, connectivity) | Clear TRAIL |
| Painted line on a field | Visual-geometric only | Marginal / metaphorical |
| Line of ants | Linear, moving -- but no substrate, no permanence | Metaphorical for humans, probably nothing for dogs |
| "Career path" | Abstract source-trajectory-goal only | Fully abstract metaphor, humans only |

**The new evidence confirms multi-property clustering:**
- Hepper & Wells 2005: Dogs require 5 sequential footsteps + 1-2s temporal decay to determine direction. Trail representation includes individual odour signature, temporal gradient, spatial discreteness, sequential order, and emergent directionality.
- Thesen et al. 1993: Three-phase tracking (search/decide/track) with controlled sampling (6 Hz sniff frequency, 1cm nose distance) demonstrates active structure extraction across 3-5 footprints. 100% success across grass and concrete confirms projectibility.
- Cafazzo et al. 2012: Free-ranging dogs concentrate marking at territorial boundaries and crossroads (z = 2.98, p = 0.003), showing functional categorization of trail network topology (nodes vs segments).

These aren't independent cues—they're a causally maintained cluster. Walking deposits odour that decays temporally while forming a spatial sequence. The painted-line case isolates the geometric subset: when only visual-geometric properties remain, the dog still recruits TRAIL. That's cross-domain extension on partial property overlap.

## The synthesis

### What HPC adds to metaphor theory
- **Stability mechanisms.** L&J describe the clusters; HPC explains why they cohere and why some properties are more central.
- **Falsification structure.** HPC's failure modes (thin, fat, negative) provide exclusion power that CMT lacks.
- **Graded membership grounded in mechanisms.** Prototype effects aren't cognitive illusions or representational artefacts; they reflect real variation in how many homeostatic properties are instantiated.

#### Added from Boyd 1979: Inductive open-endedness

**Boyd on TCM open-endedness (pp. 488-489):**
"The function of such metaphors is to put us on the track of these respects of similarity or analogy; indeed, the employment of terms in such metaphors may best be understood as contributing to features of the world delineated in terms of those – perhaps as yet undiscovered – similarities and analogies. Thus, it is hardly surprising that, at least for a time, it is not known exactly what the relevant respects of similarity or analogy are; many have yet to be discovered or understood."

**TCMs as invitations to research (p. 489):**
"Precisely because theory-constitutive metaphors are invitations to future research, and because that research is aimed at uncovering the theoretically important similarities between the primary and secondary subjects of the metaphors, the explication of these similarities and analogies is the routine business of scientific researchers, rather than of some specialized body of commentators."

**TCMs as accommodation (p. 490):**
"Theory-constitutive metaphors, in other words, represent one strategy for the accommodation of language to as yet undiscovered causal features of the world."

**Connection to HPC:** Productive metaphors exhibit inductive open-endedness precisely because they connect HPC kinds. As we discover more about the causal mechanisms in either domain, we discover new respects of similarity. The metaphor is projectible because both domains are stabilized by mechanisms, not fixed definitions.

### What metaphor theory adds to HPC
- **Cross-domain extension.** HPC has focused on within-domain categorization. Metaphor shows HPC-structured categories reaching *across* domains when partial property overlap triggers recruitment.
- **Gradience at the literal/metaphorical boundary.** The transition from peripheral literal categorization to metaphorical extension is itself fuzzy, which is a prediction HPC makes but hasn't previously been asked to deliver.

#### Added: Synthesis from three canine papers (Hepper & Wells 2005, Thesen 1993, Cafazzo 2012)

**The convergent evidence for TRAIL as HPC:**

These three papers independently confirm that canine TRAIL representation has precisely the structure HPC theory predicts for natural categories:

1. **Property clustering with causal maintenance** (Hepper & Wells 2005):
   - Dogs require 5 sequential footsteps + 1-2s temporal decay for direction determination
   - Randomized footsteps (same total odour) eliminate directional ability → sequential order is constitutive, not incidental
   - Individual odour removal (rubber overshoes) eliminates ability → property cluster includes signature + decay + sequence
   - The cluster is maintained by locomotion (deposits odour) + chemical decay (creates temporal gradient)

2. **Multi-property integration during inference** (Thesen et al. 1993):
   - Three-phase behaviour (search/decide/track) shows structured cognitive process, not reflexive stimulus-response
   - Deciding phase: dogs examine 3.0-3.1 footprints at half speed with triple-duration sniffing, maintaining 6 Hz sniff frequency and 1cm nose distance
   - 100% success across grass and concrete, 3-minute and 20-minute-old tracks → projectibility across substrate and temporal variation
   - "We suggest that during the deciding phase the dog behaves in a way that gives constant detection variables" (p. 251) → active standardization for property comparison

3. **Functional topology: nodes vs segments** (Cafazzo et al. 2012):
   - Free-ranging dogs concentrate scent-marking at crossroads along territorial boundaries (z = 2.98, p = 0.003)
   - Crossroads are topological nodes where trails intersect → TRAIL representation includes connectivity structure, not just linear geometry
   - Alpha male defecation: 81.82% at boundaries/crossroads on conspicuous substrates → strategic placement treats trail network as functionally structured space
   - Territorial boundaries function as trails (narrow linear features repeatedly traversed for marking)

**Why this is HPC, not classical categorization:**
- No single property necessary: painted lines lack odour and substrate but are still treated as trails
- No single property sufficient: odour alone without sequential structure doesn't support direction determination
- Mechanisms maintain the cluster: walking + decay + functional use hold the properties together
- Projectibility: dogs generalize across surfaces, temporal scales, and contexts (pet vs free-ranging vs wild)
- Boundary cases: painted line is marginal (visual-geometric properties only) but recruitable

**The metaphorical extension:** When Brett's dog follows a painted line, it recruits the TRAIL cluster despite instantiating only the geometric subset. This is cross-domain HPC extension—from locomotion-supporting ecological feature (source domain) to spatial demarcation artifact (target domain) on partial property overlap. The operation is phylogenetically old; what's distinctively human is deliberate exploitation and linguistic communication of the mapping.

#### Added from Lakoff 1987: Image schemas as stabilizing mechanisms

**Lakoff on image schemas (lines 10279-10281):**
"Image schemas are relatively simple structures that constantly recur in our everyday bodily experience: CONTAINERS, PATHS, LINKS, FORCES, BALANCE, and in various orientations and relations: UP-DOWN, FRONT-BACK, PART-WHOLE, CENTER-PERIPHERY, etc."

**Direct meaningfulness (lines 10286-10289):**
"These structures are directly meaningful, first, because they are directly and repeatedly experienced because of the nature of the body and its mode of functioning in our environment."

**CONTAINER schema structure (lines 10406-10448):**
- Bodily experience: body as container (ingestion/excretion); rooms, clothes, etc.
- Structural elements: INTERIOR, BOUNDARY, EXTERIOR
- Basic logic: P or not-P (in or out); modus ponens (if A in B, X in A → X in B)
- Metaphors: visual field (come into sight), relationships (trapped in marriage)

**SOURCE-PATH-GOAL schema (lines 10543-10574):**
- Bodily experience: "Every time we move anywhere there is a place we start from, a place we wind up at, a sequence of contiguous locations connecting the starting and ending points, and a direction."
- Structural elements: SOURCE, DESTINATION, PATH, DIRECTION
- Basic logic: passing through intermediate points takes time; further = more time elapsed
- Metaphors: PURPOSES ARE DESTINATIONS (going toward a goal, getting sidetracked, obstacles in the way)

**PART-WHOLE schema (lines 10482-10510):**
- Bodily experience: body as whole with manipulable parts
- Structural elements: WHOLE, PARTS, CONFIGURATION
- Basic logic: asymmetric (A part of B → B not part of A); irreflexive; configuration matters
- Metaphors: families, social organizations (marriage creates whole; divorce = splitting up)

**LINK schema (lines 10511-10521):**
- Bodily experience: umbilical cord, holding parents' hands, tying objects together
- Structural elements: two entities A and B, LINK connecting them
- Basic logic: constraint and dependence; symmetry (A linked to B ↔ B linked to A)
- Metaphors: social ties, bondage (slavery), freedom (nothing tying us down)

**CENTER-PERIPHERY schema (lines 10522-10542):**
- Bodily experience: trunk/organs (center) vs. fingers/toes/hair (periphery)
- Structural elements: ENTITY, CENTER, PERIPHERY
- Basic logic: periphery depends on center, not vice versa
- Metaphors: theories have central/peripheral principles; importance = centrality

**Structural correlations motivate metaphor (lines 10615-10620):**
"This correlation provides an answer to questions 2 and 3: VERTICALITY serves as an appropriate source domain for understanding QUANTITY because of the regular correlation in our experience between VERTICALITY and QUANTITY. The details of the mapping are motivated by the details of structural correlation cited above. Every detail of the metaphor is motivated by our physical functioning."

**HPC reframing:** Image schemas are cognitive stabilizing mechanisms. In HPC terms:
1. **Acquisition**: Schemas emerge from repeated bodily experience
2. **Entrenchment**: Constant repetition makes schemas automatic and projectable
3. **Interactive alignment**: Metaphors succeed because they exploit shared embodied schemas
4. **Iterated transmission**: Image schemas are culturally universal because they're grounded in human body structure + gravity + locomotion

Boyd says metaphors track world structure; Lakoff says metaphors track body-environment structure. Both are right—the body is part of the world, and embodied experience is causally structured.

**Lakoff on experience broadly construed (lines 10240-10245):**
"Experience is thus not taken in the narrow sense of the things that have 'happened to happen' to a single individual. Experience is instead construed in the broad sense: the totality of human experience and everything that plays a role in it—the nature of our bodies, our genetically inherited capacities, our modes of physical functioning in the world, our social organization, etc."

**Lakoff on experiential realism (lines 10207-10213):**
"Experiential realism, or experientialism, as Johnson and I (1980) called it, is a version of internal realism that attempts to provide at least some of what is needed: alternative accounts of meaning, truth, knowledge, understanding, objectivity, and reason; a theory of cognitive models capable of dealing with the facts of categorization and natural language semantics."

**What Lakoff lacks:** No theory of what maintains image-schematic structure over time across individuals, communities, and historical change. No theory of projection beyond metaphor. No theory of failure modes (what happens when image schemas conflict or fail to extend productively).

**What HPC adds:**
- Stability mechanisms: image schemas are stable because they're continuously regenerated by bodily experience + cultural transmission
- Projectibility: image schemas support induction because they track reliable body-environment regularities
- Failure modes: image schemas fail when bodily experience changes (microgravity, prosthetics, virtual environments) or when metaphorical extensions become too distant (dead metaphors, literary coinages)

### What's distinctively human
- Not the basic operation (cross-domain HPC extension on partial overlap -- dogs do this).
- But: deliberate exploitation, recursive stacking ("career path" → "roadblock on my career path" → "detour around the roadblock"), linguistic communication of the mapping, and the capacity to notice and comment on the metaphorical status of the extension.
- Connects to ostensive communication: humans can *show* that they're extending a category metaphorically, which is itself communicative work.

## Supporting evidence to develop

### Dog TRAIL as functional spatial category (beyond Brett's observations)
- Dogs distinguish trail from non-trail as functional zones (Bekoff 1997, Foltin 2021, Reid 1984)
- Trail tracking requires continuous-object representation, not point-by-point scent following (Reddy et al. 2021 sector search model)
- Elimination-zone separation is innate and develops by 1 month without training (Wagner et al. 2014)

#### Added from Hepper & Wells 2005 (directional trail tracking)

**Multi-property TRAIL representation confirmed:**
Dogs determine trail direction from odour alone, requiring information from **exactly 5 sequential footsteps** to successfully track direction (Hepper & Wells 2005, p. 296, Experiment 5). This is not simple scent-following:

**Temporal-sequential structure is critical:**
- **5 footsteps**: All 6 dogs successful (9-10/10 correct; P < 0.01)
- **3 footsteps**: All 6 dogs at chance level (3-6/10 correct)
- **Temporal decay requirement**: "It takes ∼1–2 s for the odour information in footsteps to change to provide discernible information that can be used by dogs to determine direction" (p. 291, abstract)
- **Walking calculation**: "For 5 footsteps there is a time difference of 1.9 s between the first footstep and the fifth, whereas for 3 footsteps the time difference is 0.9 s" (p. 297)

**Sequential order is mandatory:**
When footstep order was randomized (same total odour, same spatial extent), "dogs performed at the level of chance" (p. 294). "The behaviour of the dogs in experiment 2a also differed. They back-tracked much more frequently and often changed the direction in which they tracked" (p. 294).

**Individual odour, not ground disturbance:**
When individual odour was removed but ground disturbance remained (rubber + plastic overshoes), "dogs performed at the level of chance" (p. 295). "Thus dogs in this study were using the individual odour cue deposited by a footstep to determine direction" (p. 296).

**Properties clustered in canine TRAIL representation (from Hepper & Wells):**
- Individual odour signature (not just ground disturbance)
- Temporal decay gradient (fresher → older)
- Spatial discreteness (individual footsteps as units)
- Sequential order (position in series)
- Directionality (emergent from temporal gradient + sequence)

These properties are *maintained together* by the physical process of trail-laying (walking deposits odour that decays over time). This is precisely HPC structure: a cluster of properties held together by mechanisms (here, locomotion + chemical decay).

#### Added from Thesen 1993 (three-phase tracking behaviour)

**Structured cognitive process, not reflexive following:**
German shepherds exhibit three distinct behavioural phases when tracking (Thesen et al. 1993):
1. **Searching phase**: Dog tries to find the track
2. **Deciding phase**: Dog determines the direction of the track
3. **Tracking phase**: Dog follows the track in the determined direction

**100% success rate**: "In every test the dogs tracked in the correct direction (i.e. in the direction in which the track-maker had moved)" (p. 249). Tested on both grass (20-minute-old tracks) and concrete (3-minute-old tracks).

**The deciding phase reveals multi-property integration:**
- **Duration**: 3-5 seconds (mean 4.7±1.1s on grass, 5.3±2.5s on concrete)
- **Footprints examined**: 2-5 footprints (mean 3.1±1.1 on grass, 3.0±0.8 on concrete)
- **Movement speed**: Half the speed of other phases (1.9±0.5 steps/s vs 3.1-3.2 steps/s)
- **Sniffing duration**: Three times longer (35.6±15.6 sniffs per period on concrete vs 11.5-13.7 in other phases)
- **Sniff frequency**: Constant at 6 Hz across all phases (remarkably consistent)
- **Nose position**: Held ~1cm from ground during deciding phase

**Comparative processing across structured sequence:**
"We assume that the dogs determine the track direction by perceiving differences in the concentration of certain substances deposited by the track-maker. This implies that, in some of our tests, the dogs must have determined a difference in the concentration of scent in the air above two consecutive prints which were made 1s apart, either 3min or 20min earlier" (p. 251).

**Controlled sampling behaviour:**
"We suggest that during the deciding phase the dog behaves in a way that gives constant detection variables, as far as possible. These variables include the distance between the source of the scent and the nose, the volume of each sniff and the sniffing frequency" (p. 251). This is **active structure extraction**, not passive stimulus response.

**Cross-surface robustness:**
100% success across both grass and concrete, across 3-minute and 20-minute-old tracks, demonstrates projectibility: dogs are applying a general TRAIL category that abstracts over surface types and temporal scales.

**Convergence with Hepper & Wells 2005:**
Thesen et al. found dogs examine 2-5 footprints during the deciding phase (mean ~3); Hepper & Wells found 5 sequential footsteps required for reliable direction determination. The convergence is striking: both studies independently identify a **minimum structural unit** of ~3-5 discrete odour samples for directional inference. This is not moment-to-moment gradient following—it's comparative analysis across a structured temporal sequence.

### Cross-species metaphorical behaviour
- Are there other cases of animals treating non-functional instances of a category as if they were functional based on perceptual overlap?
- Possible examples: birds mobbing owl decoys (PREDATOR category extended to non-functional perceptual match); fish following artificial current lines; corvid tool use (extending STICK category to novel materials based on geometric properties)
- Need to be careful: these could all be "broad literal category" rather than cross-domain extension. The key diagnostic is whether the target object belongs to a different functional domain.

### Dennett's metaphorical triangulation
- Dennett's method of approaching a phenomenon from multiple metaphorical angles maps onto HPC diagnostics: each property cluster is a different metaphorical lens, the category emerges at convergence.
- Direct application to "noun": triangulating across morphological, syntactic, semantic, and distributional properties. No single property necessary or sufficient.

### Mitchell's ARC results (secondary connection)
- AI solves ARC puzzles through shortcuts, not core-knowledge concepts. Same-extension-different-mechanism phenomenon.
- An LLM maintains the noun/verb distinction extensionally through statistical regularities rather than the homeostatic mechanisms that maintain it in human communities. Same output, different homeostasis.
- Lakoff must call this an illusion (no embodied grounding → no genuine categorization). HPC can say something more nuanced: real extensional structure without the original homeostatic mechanisms. Genuine boundary case, not fraud.

## Empirical literature on dog trail cognition (from Elicit, 2026-02-26)

### Trail following and trail adherence
- **Boulder off-leash study**: dogs "generally did not travel far off trail, that when they did it was for short periods of time."
- **GPS tracking (n=30, 120 walks)**: dogs reliably return to and follow established routes; almost half stayed within 150m of owner on all walks.
- **Ancestral roots**: dogs inherit from wolves the behaviour of using established trails to conserve energy and mark territory with scent.
- **Thesen et al. -- three-phase olfactory tracking in German shepherds**: (1) searching phase (rapid, short-duration sniffs), (2) deciding phase (slow down, increase sniff duration upon detecting track), (3) tracking phase (steady following). Implies dogs represent trails as coherent linear objects, not point-to-point scent gradients.
- **Reddy et al. 2021 (PNAS) -- sector search strategy**: animals including dogs maintain a noisy estimate of trail heading based on recent contacts with the trail and search within an angular sector. Geometric, memory-based approach, not simple chemotaxis.

### Defecation site selection
- **Brady et al. 2019**: canines are "evolutionarily predisposed to search for particular molecules that activate gastrointestinal neurobiology and physiology via olfaction" -- pre-defecation sniffing is active olfactory assessment, not idle behaviour.
- **Wagner et al. 2014 (shelter dogs)**: dogs in double-compartment kennels placed feces on the opposite side from food/water/bedding significantly more often than chance. Elimination-separation behaviour generalizes beyond paths to functional zones more broadly.
- **Reid et al. 1984**: dogs off-lead more likely to defecate in public places, suggesting leash constraint affects spatial choice for elimination.
### What the literature confirms and what it misses
- **Confirms**: Dogs have a functional spatial category for trails; trail tracking is coherent-linear-object representation; defecation site selection discriminates trail from non-trail.
- **Misses**: Nobody has synthesized this under a "trail as functional category" framing or connected it to metaphor. The metaphor connection and the HPC framing are new.
- **The painted-line observation remains the theoretically crucial case** because it strips away olfactory and substrate explanations. The literature establishes that dogs have rich trail cognition; the painted-line case shows this category extending to a perceptually-matching but functionally-distinct object. That extension is the metaphorical move.

#### Added: Boyd + HPC → CMT Reframing (synthesis from notes)

**The argument:**

1. **Boyd:** Theory-constitutive metaphors succeed by accommodating language to causal structure; they refer to natural kinds (often HPC kinds) through open-ended epistemic access rather than definition.

2. **HPC theory:** Natural kinds are individuated by homeostatic mechanisms that maintain property clusters over time; these kinds resist essentialist definition but support reliable induction.

3. **CMT reframing:** Conceptual metaphors are **cross-domain HPC extensions**:
   - Source and target domains are both HPC kinds
   - Metaphor maps between domains that share **overlapping mechanisms**
   - Metaphorical entailments are **projectible** because mechanisms in the source domain predict properties in the target domain
   - Metaphors are **stable** across communities because mechanisms maintain property clusters in both domains
   - Metaphors exhibit **open-endedness** because HPC kinds support discovery of novel properties

**What this adds to Lakoff:**
- **Causal grounding:** Mechanisms, not just embodied schemas
- **Stability explanation:** Why metaphors persist (homeostasis)
- **Projectibility account:** Why metaphorical inferences are reliable (causal structure)
- **Failure conditions:** Why some metaphors don't work (no shared mechanisms)
- **Field-relativity:** Same phenomenon, different metaphors for different purposes (syntax vs. phonology vs. semantics may use different LANGUAGE IS X metaphors legitimately)

**What Lakoff lacks:**
- No account of **mechanisms** that maintain conceptual structure
- No explanation of **why** embodied schemas are stable across speakers
- No clear **success conditions** beyond experiential fit
- No treatment of **field-relative** projectibility

**Example: LANGUAGE IS ORGANISM succeeds** (both have growth/development mechanisms, functional adaptation, historical lineages, death/extinction). **LANGUAGE IS ROCK fails** (no shared mechanisms).

#### Added: Key quotations from Boyd 1979

**On accommodation (p. 483):**
"the use of metaphor is one of many devices available to the scientific community to accomplish the task of *accommodation of language to the causal structure of the world*. By this I mean the task of introducing terminology, and modifying usage of existing terminology, so that linguistic categories are available which describe the causally and explanatorily significant features of the world."

**On epistemic access (p. 503):**
"The central task of a theory of reference is to explain the role of language in the acquisition, assessment, improvement, and communication of knowledge, especially the role of language in making possible social cooperation and rational deliberation within these activities. *What is to be explained is our (collective) capacity to successfully detect and discover facts about the world*."

**On precision through vagueness (p. 530):**
"Vagueness in extension is by no means always indicative of any imprecision at all. For some kinds, 'vagueness' in the application of the associated terminology is precisely indicative of *precision* in the accommodation of language to the actual structure of the world."

**On HPC homeostasis (p. 526):**
"Their co-occurrence is, at least typically, the result of what may be metaphorically (sometimes literally) described as a sort of *homeostasis*. Either the presence of some of the properties in F (under appropriate conditions) to favor the presence of the others, or there are underlying mechanisms or processes which tend to maintain the presence of the properties in F, or both."

#### Added: Japanese *hon* classifier (Lakoff 1987, lines 4260-4509)

**Extension via image-schema transformations:**
1. Central members: rigid long thin objects (sticks, pencils, bamboo)
2. Extensions via trajectory ↔ long thin object (baseball hits, telephone calls via wires)
3. Metonymy: principal functional object → principal goal (bat → hit; needle → injection)
4. Conventional mental images: rolls of tape (unrolled = functional = long and thin)
5. Category chaining: letters → telephone calls → radio/TV → movies (all communication via CONDUIT metaphor)

**HPC connection:** Image-schema transformations and metonymies are cognitive mechanisms that maintain category coherence. The *hon* category is a property cluster (rigid + long + thin + functional salience) held together by embodied image schemas and cultural conventions.

## Paper-by-paper findings (Sonnet agents, 2026-02-26)

### Murphy 1996: On Metaphoric Representation

**Core move:** Distinguishes strong CMT (concepts literally structured by metaphoric relations to source domains) from weak CMT (metaphors influence but don't constitute). Rejects strong view as incoherent; proposes **structural similarity** as alternative.

**Key arguments for us:**
- **Multiple metaphors problem:** LOVE IS A JOURNEY + LOVE IS WAR + LOVE IS A PATIENT create incoherence under strong CMT: "it is difficult to see how these two metaphors could simultaneously inform the same concept" (p. 186). HPC dissolves this: each metaphor extends a different property subset; none is essential.
- **Homunculus regress:** If conceptual links are metaphoric, who interprets the metaphor? "If the link itself is metaphoric, then the theory seems to be appealing to a homunculus who interprets the metaphor" (p. 182).
- **Structural similarity is proto-HPC:** "concepts such as love, journey, disease, insanity, anger, and so on, are represented separately, with their own structures" (p. 195). Each metaphor "simply picks out different aspects of the concept's content" (p. 196). These are independent property clusters with alignable relational structure.
- **Asymmetry problem:** LOVE uses JOURNEY metaphors but not vice versa. HPC explains: source domains have stronger homeostatic maintenance (journey-as-physical-motion stabilised by perception, action, cultural routines); target domains borrow from better-maintained sources.
- **Conventionalisation challenge:** "metaphors become increasingly conventional, there will be a shift in mode of alignment from comparison to categorization" (p. 199). Murphy notes CMT lacks an explicit causal maintenance model; HPC supplies it.

### Gentner 1983: Structure-Mapping Theory

**Core move:** Analogy = mapping relational structure from base to target, discarding attributes. The **systematicity principle**: interconnected higher-order relational systems preferred over isolated matches.

**Key arguments for us:**
- **Systematicity = homeostatic coherence.** "Relations that belong to a mappable system of mutually interconnecting relationships is more likely to be imported into the target than is an isolated predicate" (p. 163). HPC: mechanisms are systems of mutually reinforcing causal processes.
- **Higher-order relations constrain lower-order ones.** Solar system/atom: MORE MASSIVE THAN maps because it participates in the central-force system; HOTTER THAN doesn't because it's isolated (p. 164). HPC: mechanisms (higher-order causal relations) constrain which properties cluster.
- **Relations, not attributes.** "The strength of an analogical match does not seem to depend on the overall degree of featural overlap" (p. 156). HPC parallel: what transfers in cross-domain extension is relational structure (mechanisms), not intrinsic properties.

### Bowdle & Gentner 2005: Career of Metaphor

**Core move:** Novel metaphors processed as comparisons (structural alignment); conventional metaphors as categorisations (abstract category membership). Repeated use → abstraction → polysemy → lexicalisation.

**Key arguments for us:**

**Career of metaphor = HPC stabilisation trajectory (4 stages):**
1. **Novel metaphor** (comparison) = exploratory HPC extension. Costly structural alignment tests whether source-domain mechanisms can apply to target.
2. **Conventional metaphor** (categorisation) = HPC category membership. Abstract category (generalised mechanisms) applies directly. Faster: M = 2,063ms conventional vs 3,058ms novel (Experiment 2).
3. **Dead₁ metaphor** = HPC category with severed historical link. Base term homonymous, not polysemous.
4. **Dead₂ metaphor** = HPC category split. Two independent categories, historically related, synchronically distinct (e.g., *culture* 'agriculture' vs 'intellectual domain').

**Conventionalisation = mechanism generalisation:**
- "the career of metaphor hypothesis states that aligning the literal target and base concepts of a metaphor can lead to the induction and eventual lexicalization of domain-general relational schemas" (p. 197)
- Experiment 3: repeated exposure to novel metaphors shifts processing from simile-preference to metaphor-preference (comparison → categorisation)

**What HPC adds that Bowdle & Gentner lack:** *Why* does conventionalisation happen for some metaphors and not others? HPC answer: when the relational structure (mechanisms) can be maintained in the target domain. If the target lacks mechanism support, the metaphor stays novel or dies.

### Holyoak & Stamenkovic 2018: Metaphor Comprehension Review

**Core move:** Multi-mechanism integration. Simple metaphors → categorisation; complex metaphors → analogy. Against strong CMT: "none of the many neural studies of metaphor processing revealed clear activation of the RLPFC, the region that has emerged as a neural marker of complex analogical reasoning" (p. 653).

**Key arguments for us:**
- **Aptness > conventionality** as processing predictor (Jones & Estes 2006). HPC reframing: aptness = degree to which source-domain mechanisms can be maintained in target domain. Apt metaphors = high mechanism transferability.
- **Working memory predicts complex metaphor comprehension** (Chiappe & Chiappe 2007). HPC: checking whether multi-component mechanisms can co-adapt to a new domain requires holding multiple property-mechanism relations in WM.
- **Career of metaphor "called into serious question"** (p. 652) as universal story. But fits HPC perfectly: stability is graded and mechanism-dependent, not a uniform trajectory.
- **Against strong CMT embodiment:** "does not support the extreme view that comprehension of expressions ordinarily considered literal, such as 'I'm depressed,' necessarily requires access to a conceptual mapping such as SAD IS DOWN" (p. 654).

### Field 1973: Theory Change and the Indeterminacy of Reference

**Core move:** Scientific terms can be referentially indeterminate -- partially denoting multiple quantities. Newton's 'mass' partially denoted both proper mass and relativistic mass, with no fact of the matter about which.

**Key arguments for us:**
- **Partial denotation:** "a singular term can partially denote more than one thing" (p. 475). Parallel: metaphors partially denote across domains; HPC categories partially instantiate property clusters.
- **Against pure causal theories:** Causal contact alone doesn't determine reference. HPC addition: mechanisms (not just causal baptism) stabilise reference.
- **Reference is graded, not binary:** "partial denotation and partial signification... are perfectly objective relations between words and extralinguistic objects" (p. 481). Maps onto HPC boundary cases where items instantiate some but not all cluster properties.

### Cummiskey 1992: Reference Failure and Scientific Realism

**Core move:** Pure causal theories can't explain reference *failure*. Needs hybrid account: causal contact + Boyd's theory-constitutive metaphors as flexible descriptive constraints.

**Key arguments for us (strongest single paper for the argument):**

**4 success conditions for metaphorical reference** (p. 39):
1. Quasi-perception provides causal link to world
2. Theory-constitutive metaphor provides link to underlying natural property/kind
3. Resulting research programme is phenomenally successful
4. Explanatory metaphor is fruitfully articulated

**Mechanism articulation is the key discriminator:**
- Caloric theory: phenomenal success (heat transfer experiments), but caloric-as-fluid metaphor couldn't be articulated → reference failure (p. 34)
- Kinetic theory: phenomenal success AND metaphor articulable (molecular motion) → reference success
- "When a new explanatory mechanism can explain the successes and can be more fully articulated, it is reasonably concluded that no explanatory significant feature of the world was picked out by the central term of the theory" (p. 35)

**Inductive open-endedness = mechanism discovery:**
"Theory constitutive metaphors are 'invitations to future research': they serve to introduce terms without specifying the defining characteristics of the referent of the term, but instead leave it open for the routine business of scientists to discover the yet unspecified essential properties" (p. 28)

**Reference failure = mechanism failure:** When the metaphor can't be articulated, there's no underlying mechanism maintaining the presumed property cluster. HPC: metaphors fail when the partial overlap is superficial, not sustained by homeostatic mechanisms.

### Glynn 2010: Objectivity and Verification in Usage-Based Cognitive Semantics

**Core move:** Even highly subjective semantic analysis can be operationalised, replicated, and statistically tested. Case study of *bother*: form-meaning pairings cluster, not discretely.

**Key arguments for us:**
- **Semantic features cluster with constructions:** Correspondence Analysis reveals Oblique Transitive + 'interruption'/'imposition' as one cluster; Direct Transitive + 'physical pain'/'emotional pain'/'anxiety' as another (pp. 251-253). HPC: form-meaning pairings maintained by usage mechanisms.
- **Against discrete senses:** "lexical senses are not discrete, but are often drawn towards certain formal variants of a word's use and should not be seen as pockets of meaning inside words waiting to be activated by situation-context" (p. 256). Directly supports HPC gradient categories.
- **Inter-coder agreement excellent** for subjective features: Affect κ = 0.949, Agent κ = 0.988, Theme κ = 0.937, Cause κ = 0.951. Shows HPC property clusters are intersubjectively detectable.
- **Methodological toolkit** for testing whether metaphorical mappings create stable property clusters (as HPC predicts) vs ad hoc extensions.

### Noyes & Keil 2019: Generics Designate Kinds But Not Always Essences

**Core move:** Generics signal "kindhood" (rich causal structure, inductive potential) separable from "essentialism" (internal natural causes). Four studies (n=739).

**Key arguments for us:**
- **Kindhood without essentialism** (the whole point): "Kindhood does not imply essentialism, and essentialism is not necessary to kindhood" (p. 2). Directly supports HPC: projectible categories without underlying essences.
- **Property content determines causal interpretation:** Cultural generics → kind assumption but NOT essence assumption (d = -0.30). Biological generics → both (essence d = 0.70). Neutral generics → kind (d = 0.52-1.14) but not essence (all p > 0.09). HPC: the mechanism type varies, but the clustering is real.
- **Generics as cultural transmission mechanism:** "generics are a subtle and ordinary linguistic cue by which the kind assumption can be transmitted within a culture" (p. 5). Feedback loop: kind assumption → generic use → kind assumption. HPC stabiliser.
- **Diverse kinds:** "tigers, oak trees, chairs, money, artists, and Christians. They vary considerably in their underlying causal structure, including whether they are natural or social (or both) and whether they have or lack an essence" (p. 2).

### Lakoff & Johnson 1980/2003: Metaphors We Live By

**Core move:** Abstract thought fundamentally metaphorical; mappings grounded in embodied experience. 2003 Afterword adds neural theory: primary metaphors from conflated childhood experience, neural recruitment, stable conventional system.

**Key arguments for us:**

**Experiential gestalts = HPCs.** L&J on causation: "a cluster of other components... a whole that we human beings find more basic than the parts" (pp. 69-70). Twelve properties characterising prototypical causation emerge "through their constant recurrence in our everyday functioning" (pp. 71-72). This IS an HPC: property cluster, emergent unity, family-resemblance structure.

**Partial mapping is central to L&J, not an embarrassment:**
- "The metaphorical structuring involved here is partial, not total. If it were total, one concept would actually be the other, not merely be understood in terms of it" (p. 13)
- "Mappings tend to be partial... A source domain element is not mapped if it would produce an inference that would contradict the internal structure of the target" (2003 Afterword, p. 254)
- Neural mechanism for blocking: "If a metaphorical link would result in a contradiction in the target domain, it will not be learned. Neurally, contradictions are mutual inhibitions" (2003, p. 259)

**Stability mechanisms in the 2003 Afterword:**
1. Neural recruitment: "Neurons that fire together wire together" (p. 256)
2. Stable conventional system: "This neural learning mechanism produces a stable, conventional system of primary metaphors that tend to remain in place indefinitely" (p. 256)
3. Embodied grounding: universal experiences → universal primary metaphors (p. 259)
4. Inference preservation: "Image-schema structure and image-schematic inferences seemed to be 'preserved' by metaphors" (p. 253)
5. Cultural transmission (implicit throughout; coherence discussion pp. 21-24)

**Multiple metaphors = multiple stabilisers:** Love structured by Journey, War, Patient metaphors -- each highlights different aspect, none exhaustive (p. 86). This IS HPC's anti-essentialism.

**The gap stated precisely:** Boyd explains WHY metaphors succeed (causal structure) but not HOW they're maintained. Lakoff explains HOW metaphors arise (embodiment, neural recruitment) but not what makes mappings STABLE vs EPHEMERAL. HPC fills both gaps.

## Key theoretical synthesis (from paper consolidation)

### Three debates reframed by HPC

**1. Comparison vs categorisation** (Gentner vs Glucksberg):
Both are stages of HPC extension. Comparison (novel) = testing mechanism transferability across domains. Categorisation (conventional) = membership in generalised HPC category. Career of metaphor = HPC stabilisation trajectory.

**2. Attribute vs relational mapping** (Murphy, Gentner):
Relations = mechanisms; attributes = properties. Only mechanism-supported properties transfer. Systematicity (Gentner) = homeostatic coherence (HPC).

**3. Embodied cognition vs abstract structure** (Lakoff vs Murphy/Gentner):
Embodied schemas are one mechanism type (sensorimotor). HPC allows multiple stabilisers: perceptual, motor, social, functional, cultural, institutional. Lakoff's "conceptual mappings" = conventionalised HPC categories (generalised mechanisms).

### The synthesis in one paragraph

CMT describes the mapping structure. Boyd identifies accommodation to causal structure as the success condition. HPC specifies what that causal structure is: property clusters maintained by homeostatic mechanisms. Metaphor = cross-domain HPC extension on partial property overlap. Novel metaphors test mechanism transferability (comparison/analogy); conventional metaphors apply generalised mechanisms (categorisation). Metaphors succeed when source-domain mechanisms can be maintained in the target domain; they fail when the overlap is superficial and no mechanisms sustain it (Cummiskey's caloric, phlogiston). Reference is graded (Field's partial denotation), not binary; this maps onto HPC boundary cases where items instantiate some but not all cluster properties.

### New open questions from the papers

- Can aptness (Holyoak & Stamenkovic) be operationalised as mechanism transferability?
- Cummiskey's "phenomenal success" vs "articulation success" -- can these be distinguished empirically for everyday metaphors (not just scientific ones)?
- L&J's experiential gestalts (12-property causation) are HPC candidates. Can the HPC diagnostic (mechanisms + projectibility) be applied to them directly?
- Dead metaphors as HPC category splits: can we find cases where the split tracks mechanism divergence?
- How does this connect to Peirce's interpretant/habit framework? L&J's "experiential gestalt" resonates with habit; "cross-domain correlation in experience" could be mediation (not brute correlation but interpreted relation). Metaphor = new interpretant for a sign?

## Expanded canine evidence (from mining downloaded papers)

### Geometric trail representation (Reddy et al. 2021)

This is the strongest theoretical framework for the claim that dogs have a functional representation of TRAIL. The sector search model requires the animal to maintain:

1. An estimate of the trail's heading (direction)
2. An estimate of uncertainty about that heading
3. Memory of at least 2 recent contact points
4. A model of trail statistics (persistence, curvature)

This is not "follow the smell" -- it's a geometric, predictive representation of a linear spatial feature. The casting behaviour (wide oscillations when contact is lost) is specifically a search over the *trail* category -- the animal presupposes that the trail is a continuous linear entity.

**Key detail:** Hepper & Wells (2005, cited in Reddy et al.) showed dogs determine trail direction from approximately 5 footsteps. This demonstrates directional inference from limited samples, not moment-to-moment gradient following.

### Three-zone functional model of space

Across all six canine papers, dogs consistently treat space as having at least three functional zones:

| Zone | Behavioural signature | Evidence |
|------|----------------------|----------|
| Trail/corridor | Linear travel, forward progress, speed | Bekoff 1997 (93% within 5m), Foltin 2021 (7 travel patterns around trail axis) |
| Margin/exploration | Brief lateral excursions, sniffing, marking, elimination | Bekoff 1997, Reid 1984, Foltin 2021 |
| Den/rest area | Sleep, eat, drink; elimination suppressed | Wagner 2014 (72.5% opposite-side placement, n=579) |

### Specific statistics from the papers

- **Bekoff & Meaney 1997**: 93% of dogs remained within 5m of trail at Mt. Sanitas (n=100)
- **Foltin & Ganslosser 2021**: 7 distinct spatial-movement patterns, all organized relative to the owner's route as reference axis; all 30 dogs returned without being called; median max distance 652m (known areas) vs. 434m (unknown)
- **Wagner et al. 2014**: opposite-side feces placement 72.5%, urine 77.4% (n=579 dogs, 4,440 observation-days); preference develops by 1 month of age without training
- **Reid et al. 1984**: on-lead dogs suppress elimination; off-lead dogs eliminate preferentially (defecation chi-square = 5.04, p = 0.02; urination chi-square = 20.11, p < 0.01)
- **Cafazzo et al. 2012**: Free-ranging dogs (Rome, n=27, 282.53h observation, 782 urinations). Males scent-marked territorial boundaries at higher frequency than interior (z = 3.51, p = 0.0004); crossroads along borders preferentially marked (t = 5.00, z = 2.98, p = 0.003). Alpha male defecation: 81.82% at boundaries/crossroads, 77.78% on conspicuous substrates.
### Leash constraint as experimental manipulation

Reid et al. 1984 provides a quasi-experimental finding: dogs on leads *attempt* to stop and eliminate but are pulled onward by owners (p. 286). Off-lead dogs immediately leave the trail to eliminate. The leash functions as a constraint that prevents the trail-avoidance rule from firing. This is useful because it shows the behaviour is actively motivated, not just a passive substrate preference.

### Wild canid scat-deposition evidence (from deep-research review, 2026-02-26)

Source: structured review of canid scat-deposition literature (wolves, coyotes, foxes, dogs), drawing on ~12 primary studies with full spatial methodology. Key primary sources: Vilà et al. (NW Spain wolves, n=311 scats, DOI 10.1007/s00114-004-0557-1); Barja et al. (Iberian wolf territory zonation, n=111 scats, PDF at ivb.cz); Theuerkauf et al. (Polish primeval forest, radio + snow tracking); Barja & List (coyote grasslands, road vs cross-country); Monclús et al. (Portugal wolf-dog comparison, 2011-2020, n=233 genetically confirmed scats).

**1. Wild/domestic divergence strengthens the argument.**
Wild canids (wolves, coyotes) deposit scats preferentially *on* trails and at junctions -- the opposite of domestic pet dogs, which leave the trail to defecate. But both behaviours require the animal to **discriminate trail from non-trail** and apply different rules to each zone. Whether the rule is "mark it" (wolves) or "avoid contaminating it" (pet dogs), the TRAIL category is presupposed.

**Cafazzo et al. 2012 bridges wild and domestic:** Free-ranging dogs in Rome exhibited territorial boundary marking patterns intermediate between wild canids and pet dogs. Like wolves, they concentrated marking at territorial boundaries and crossroads (males: z = 2.98, p = 0.003 for crossroads). The alpha male deposited 81.82% of defecations at boundaries/crossroads on conspicuous substrates—wolf-like strategic placement. But unlike owned pet dogs, these free-ranging dogs had access to large territory (22.14 ha) and maintained pack structure with territorial defense. The study confirms that spatial discrimination of trails and junctions is robust across domestic/wild canid populations when ecological conditions support territorial behaviour.

**2. Trails have internal structure: the junction effect.**
Wolves deposit 60.1% of scats at crossroads (Vilà et al., NW Spain) and prefer the *centre* of the trail. TRAIL isn't just "linear feature" -- it has topology. Nodes (junctions) are categorically distinct from segments. This implies the TRAIL representation includes connectivity structure, not just geometric shape.

**3. Wolf-dog convergence in rural Portugal.**
Free-ranging dogs and wolves "did not differ significantly" in scat-deposition patterns along roads/trails (Monclús et al., 2011-2020, genetically confirmed). This suggests the pet-dog "leave the trail" behaviour is a domestication/urbanization overlay. The underlying TRAIL representation is shared across wild and domestic canids; what varies is the behavioural rule applied to it.

**4. Context modulation within the same animal.**
Iberian wolves near dens deposit scats at high density but inconspicuously (ground-level, 8.3% on conspicuous substrates); away from dens, scats become conspicuous and elevated (67.3% conspicuous substrates, 34.5% above ground), concentrated at junctions (Barja et al.). The same animal applies different marking rules to TRAIL depending on functional context (den-protection vs territorial signalling). This is categorical behaviour, not a fixed response.

#### Added from Lakoff 1987: Species as non-classical categories

**Lakoff on biological species (lines 7281-7284):**
"According to evolutionary biology, species are not natural kinds in the technical objectivist sense, namely, classical categories defined by common essential properties. In fact, species in evolutionary biology are not classical categories at all."

**Seven ways species fail to be natural kinds (lines 7332-7494):**
- No homogeneous structure (statistical variation)
- Defined relationally (reproductive isolation)
- Not defined by individual properties (gene pool)
- No clear boundaries (incipient speciation)
- Non-transitive (ring species: A↔B, B↔C, C↔D, D↔E, but A≠E)
- No necessary conditions (morphology ≠ interbreeding)
- Geography-dependent (habitat changes alter species status)

**Prototype effects in biology (lines 7380-7386):**
"Biological species show prototype effects. Populations that are best examples of the biological species concept have all three of these characteristics [reproductive isolation, ecological difference, morphological distinguishability]. But the biological world is sufficiently complex that a clear same-or-different species judgment cannot be given in a great many cases."

**Phenetic species = dense regions in hyperspace (Sokal & Crovello 1984, lines 7525-7527):**
"Insistence on a phenetic species concept leads inevitably to a conceptualization of species as dense regions within a hyperdimensional environmental space." This is almost exactly Boyd's HPC formulation (property space clustering).

**Critical observation:** Lakoff does NOT cite Boyd. The book predates Boyd's 1989 "Realism, Anti-Foundationalism, and the Enthusiasm for Natural Kinds." Lakoff argues species aren't classical natural kinds and concludes objectivism fails. Boyd argues species ARE natural kinds—just HPC kinds, not classical ones.

**The reframing:** Lakoff's radial categories are linguistic HPCs; biological species are biological HPCs. Both are property clusters maintained by mechanisms. CMT describes the cognitive mechanisms (image schemas, metaphor, metonymy); HPC describes the general framework (property clustering + homeostasis).

#### Added: Radial categories ARE HPCs (structural mapping)

**From Lakoff 1987 radial category analysis:**

Radial categories have all the hallmarks of HPC kinds:
- **Property cluster**: No necessary/sufficient conditions; members share overlapping properties (line 3954: "There is no reason to believe that the Dyirbal find anything in common among women, fire, dangerous things, etc.")
- **Centrality**: Central members have the most properties; peripheral members fewer (graded membership)
- **Chaining**: Links between members via experiential domains, metonymies, image schemas
- **Mechanisms** (Lakoff's terms → HPC terms):
  - Domain-of-experience principle = functional salience + entrenchment
  - Myth-and-belief principle = cultural transmission + narrative scaffolding
  - Image-schema transformations = embodied cognition (body → mind mapping)
  - Metonymy = associative learning (contiguity → conceptual link)
- **Motivation without prediction**: "The general principles given make sense of Dyirbal classification, but they do not predict exactly what the categories will be" (line 3960) — same as HPC
- **Failure mode**: Language death shows gradual loss of peripheral members when mechanisms (cultural transmission, daily use) weaken

**Key gap in Lakoff:** No explicit theory of what **maintains** radial structure over time. He describes chaining principles but doesn't theorize stability mechanisms. HPC fills this gap: categories are stable because mechanisms (acquisition, entrenchment, interactive alignment, iterated transmission) continuously regenerate the property cluster.
